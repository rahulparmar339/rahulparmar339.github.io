CRISPR/Cas9

what is CRISPR?


CRISPR is one kind of defence mechanism in some of bacteria, it was first observed in 1980. It's part of immune system of bacteria. One DNA sequence would be repeated over and over again, and this DNA sequence was carrying an unique sequences in between those repeated sequence. This odd configuration is called “Clustered Regularly Interspaced Short Palindromic Repeats,” or CRISPR. There is also one enzyme called cas(CRISPR-associated protein) which is also part of CRISPR system.

How does CRISPR/cas9 works to protect against viral infection in bacteria?


Whenever infectious virus attack bacteria it joins in between DNA of bacteria. In bacteria CRISPR/cas9 will be automatically produced. CRISPR is actually guide RNA(gRNA) which will guide what defence mechanism to viral DNA sequence. And then gRNA will join with viral DNA sequence (gRNA is actually complementary sequence of viral DNA), cas9 enzyme with bind with joined part and it will cut viral sequence from bacteria DNA using molecular scissors. And then bacteria will add some mutation to that gap. This way CRISPR/cas9 helps to remove viral sequence.
